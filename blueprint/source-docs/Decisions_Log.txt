2. Dispute & Refund Policy
DECISION: No cash refunds on collected food. Platform credit S$10–15 for verified objective failures only. Reviews and ratings handle all subjective complaints.

Rationale
	•	Food is perishable — once collected it cannot be returned or resold.
	•	Subjective complaints ('didn't taste as expected', 'portion too small') are not grounds for refund.
	•	Cook delivered in good faith. Rating system is the primary and appropriate recourse.

Platform Credit — Three Qualifying Scenarios
	•	Foreign object in food — photo submitted within 2 hours of collection.
	•	Food clearly undercooked posing objective food safety risk — photo within 2 hours.
	•	Completely wrong dish delivered — documented with photo within 2 hours.

Credit Policy
	•	Credit S$10–15 funded entirely by platform from commission. Cook earnings NOT clawed back unless fraud confirmed.
	•	Credit applies to next order only — not cash equivalent.
	•	Platform decision is final, not subject to appeal.
	•	To include in: Cook Agreement, Customer Terms, Cook onboarding guide, Platform FAQ.

14. Food Safety, Hygiene Standards & Legal Disclaimer
DECISION: Two-layer approach — legal protection (active acknowledgement disclaimer) AND genuine operational standards. Self-certification model: platform facilitates, does not inspect or certify.

DRAFT DISCLAIMER — Singapore food law specialist review required before launch: 'Food on Singapore Home Cooks is prepared by independent home-based cooks in private kitchens. Singapore Home Cooks is a technology platform connecting home cooks with customers. We are not a food operator, we do not prepare food, and we do not inspect cooking premises. All cooks hold valid SFA registration and WSQ Food Safety certification, but preparation standards vary and are not controlled by Singapore Home Cooks. By ordering, you acknowledge you are purchasing from an independent home cook and that Singapore Home Cooks bears no liability for food quality, allergen content, food safety outcomes, or any illness, injury, or damage.'

Allergen Disclosure — Mandatory Field
	•	Required on every listing: nuts/peanuts, shellfish/seafood, dairy, gluten/wheat, eggs, soy, halal/non-halal, 'may contain'. Displayed ABOVE order button.
	•	False allergen information = grounds for immediate permanent removal. Stated explicitly in Cook Agreement.

Operational Standards
	•	WSQ Food Safety Level 1 mandatory. Platform sponsors S$30 fee. Recovery clause: deducted from payouts if cook earns less than S$150 in 6 months.
	•	Singapore Home Cooks Hygiene Guide: practical visual guide at onboarding.
	•	Cook Hygiene Declaration: signed and timestamped at onboarding.

Proactive Quality Check-In
	•	India assistant contacts every cook at 50-order milestones with warm check-in message.
	•	'Congratulations on your 50th order! How are things going? Any questions we can help with?'
	•	Surfaces issues before rating system catches them. 10 minutes per milestone.

16. Dish Search, YouTube Request & Contextual Meal Search
DECISION: Three discovery modes beyond browse. Dish name search with Singapore synonym map. YouTube recipe request with Cook's Interpretation framing. Contextual meal search: structured first, natural language AI layer second.

Dish Name Search
	•	Singapore food vocabulary synonym map (100+ pairs): 'chicken rice' finds all variants, 'laksa' finds all styles.
	•	Results: price, rating, earliest slot, calorie estimate, dietary tags. Sort by relevance, rating, or availability.
	•	'No results' converts to recipe request — never a dead end.

YouTube Recipe Request
	•	Customer pastes YouTube URL → platform auto-embeds preview and extracts title.
	•	Cook's Note mandatory in every bid: experience with dish, adaptations, presentation expectations.
	•	'Cook's Interpretation' framing throughout — inspired by, not exact recreation. No copyright concern — links only.

Contextual Meal Search
	•	'Thai food for 2, budget S$50, under 500 calories per person' — assembles complete meal packages.
	•	Single-cook combinations prioritised. Budget intelligence shows slightly-over-budget options with delta.
	•	Build structured search first (dropdowns). Add Claude API natural language layer on top once algorithm is solid.
	•	No dead ends: failed search routes to alternative cuisines or recipe request.

18. Flexible & Dynamic Pricing — Three Mechanisms
DECISION: Three cook-controlled pricing mechanisms. No algorithmic surge pricing. All transparent to customers. Real-time net earnings display on all pricing forms.

1. Festive Demand-Based Pricing
	•	Cook adjusts prices manually during peak periods. Platform sends demand alert 6 weeks before each occasion.
	•	No algorithmic surge — Singapore consumers sensitive to festive price gouging.

2. Pre-Order vs Last-Minute Pricing
	•	Cook sets last-minute premium percentage (suggested 15%) for orders within configurable window (e.g. within 4 hours).
	•	Displayed transparently: 'Pre-order for tomorrow: S$12.00 / Order now — last-minute: S$13.80 (+15%)'
	•	Incentivises advance ordering. Improves capacity planning. Reduces cancellations.

3. Occasion & Event Premium Listings
	•	Separate listing type for birthday packages, festive spreads, corporate events. Package-based pricing.
	•	Minimum advance notice set by cook. Appear in dedicated occasion pages.

20. SFA Registration — Frictionless Onboarding Design
DECISION: SFA registration requirement STAYS mandatory. The barrier is in the presentation, not the requirement. Removing the requirement would collapse the platform's legal protection. Redesign the onboarding experience to feel easy, guided, and positive.

Why Registration Cannot Be Optional
	•	Platform's entire legal protection rests on: 'We connect independent, SFA-registered food businesses with customers.'
	•	Removing registration = knowingly facilitating illegal food sales = significant liability exposure.
	•	SFA registration is not a big barrier: S$33 for 3 years, online application, under 1 hour. Designed specifically for home-based food sellers.
	•	WSQ course (1 full day, S$30 — platform sponsored) is the bigger actual friction point.

Five-Stage Frictionless Onboarding Flow
	•	Warm welcome — cook signs up immediately. Starts building profile and listings right away. SFA not mentioned yet.
	•	Step-by-step GoBusiness guide built into platform — 'Let's get your free SFA registration — about 20 minutes.' Platform opens GoBusiness, guides every click.
	•	Pending state — not blocked state. Profile complete, listings drafted, everything ready. Dashboard shows: 'Add your SFA number to go live.'
	•	WSQ course — 'We pay for it. Here's how to enrol. SkillsFuture credits may cover it entirely for Singaporeans.'
	•	Go-live celebration — animated congratulations when both SFA and WSQ uploaded and verified.

Positioning Language
	•	NOT: 'You need SFA registration to join.' YES: 'We'll walk you through getting your official home food business registration — it takes about 20 minutes and makes you a verified professional. We pay for your food safety certification. You just cook.'
	•	For cooks already registered (many active sellers are): make upload as easy as possible. OCR to auto-read registration number. These cooks join fastest and their testimony helps hesitant newcomers.

23. Peak Season Capacity Planner
DECISION: Add Peak Season Capacity Planner to Academy. Cook makes one calm capacity decision 6 weeks before each peak — not reactive decisions under pressure.

How It Works
	•	Triggered 6 weeks before each major festive occasion (same trigger as demand calendar alerts).
	•	Shows cook her order history from same period last year OR platform-wide demand estimates for her cuisine type.
	•	'Based on platform demand, cooks offering [your cuisine] typically receive approximately X orders in the 2 weeks before [occasion].'
	•	'At your current capacity of Y portions/day, you can fulfil approximately Z orders.'
	•	'Would you like to set a maximum orders cap for this period?' — cook sets number, system auto-pauses new orders when reached.
	•	Cook can adjust cap at any time. Decision made once, calmly, in advance.

Burnout Warning Notification
	•	Fires when weekly confirmed orders exceed 2× the cook's typical baseline during a festive period.
	•	Single-tap dismiss or single-tap activate pause. Fires once per peak period maximum.

29C. Platform Flexibility & Agility Architecture
DECISION: Commission rules live in a database table — never in code. Five agile architectural principles ensure new features can be added without breaking existing ones. All configurable values live in platform_config table — changed via admin interface, no redeployment needed.

Flexible Commission Engine
	•	Every commission rule: name, type, conditions (JSON), rate, priority, effective dates, active flag, audit trail. Code reads rules — never contains them.
	•	Engine evaluates all matching active rules → sorts by priority → applies lowest rate in cook's favour → enforces S$2.00 floor → returns rate + rule name for audit trail.
	•	Seven rule types: order value tier, cook recognition tier, cook designation (Founding Cook), promotional (time-bounded with auto-activation/deactivation), cuisine/occasion type, customer loyalty tier, minimum floor.
	•	CEO dashboard commission admin: create rules, edit (creates new version — never modifies original), deactivate, schedule future rules, preview 'what rate applies to this order today?'
	•	Real-time earnings display on all cook pricing forms reads from commission engine — updates automatically when rules change.

Five Agile Architectural Principles
	•	Feature flags: every new feature launched behind a flag. Toggle on/off without code deployment. Test with specific users before full rollout. CEO dashboard feature flags panel.
	•	Plugin-style modules: each feature has own tables, own API routes, own components. Adding a feature means adding a module — existing code untouched.
	•	Event-driven automation: significant actions fire events (order.collected, cook.registered, etc.). Any automation listens. Adding new automation never touches event-firing code.
	•	Configuration tables: every value that might change lives in platform_config key-value table. Change one row, effect is immediate. No redeployment.
	•	Migration discipline: schema changes via Supabase migrations. New features only add tables — never modify existing ones. Existing data never at risk.
	•	AI prompt management: all AI prompts in database (ai_prompts table). Update any AI feature through admin interface. Version history retained. A/B test prompts without code changes.

29F. Comprehensive Receipt Architecture
DECISION: Three document types — individual receipt, corporate tax invoice, and cook payout statement. All transparent about commission split. Separate sequential counters for order numbers and receipt numbers. Receipt counter resets annually. Cook SFA number captured at transaction time for historical accuracy. Credit notes for adjustments — original receipts never modified.

Key Design Decisions
	•	Commission transparency: receipt shows split — 'Amount to cook: S$19.68, Platform service fee (18%): S$4.32, Total charged: S$24.00'. Transparency builds trust. Opacity creates resentment.
	•	Separate receipt counter: order numbers (SHC-) and receipt numbers (RCP-) use independent sequential counters. Receipt counter only increments on confirmed payment — no gaps in receipt sequence = clean audit trail.
	•	Receipt number format: RCP-YYYY-NNNNN (annual counter, resets 1 January). Corporate invoice: INV-YYYY-NNNNN (same counter, different prefix). Credit note: CN-YYYY-NNNNN.
	•	Cook SFA number captured at transaction time — not linked to current profile. Ensures historical receipts remain accurate even if cook's SFA number changes.
	•	Receipts generated on demand — not pre-stored. Data retained permanently for re-generation. 5-year retention minimum per IRAS requirements.

29G. GST Compliance Framework — Singapore
CRITICAL UNRESOLVED QUESTION requiring tax specialist input before launch: What is Singapore Home Cooks' taxable turnover for GST purposes — gross GMV (Option A, principal basis) or commission revenue only (Option B, agent basis)? Option A = early registration. Option B = threshold reached much later. Answer depends on agent vs principal determination. Material financial and pricing implications. Written tax specialist opinion required.

Three-Phase Compliance Framework
Phase 1 — Before Registration
	•	Track taxable turnover in real time: CEO dashboard GST Readiness Report with running total, 75% warning flag, and projected threshold crossing date. Built from day one — missing 30-day compulsory registration window = back-tax + penalties + interest.
	•	All receipts include: 'GST not applicable — Singapore Home Cooks is not currently GST-registered.' Already in receipt architecture.
	•	Maintain 5-year accounting records. Already satisfied by double-entry ledger and receipt retention policy.
	•	Consider voluntary registration at Month 6 if significant GST-registered corporate customers — they can claim input tax credits, making platform more attractive for corporate procurement.

Phase 2 — At the Threshold
	•	CEO dashboard flags 75% of S$1M: engage accountant immediately to confirm threshold calculation and file GST registration application with IRAS through myTax Portal. IRAS processing: 2–4 weeks typical.
	•	Update platform_config table (zero code changes needed): gst_enabled: true, gst_rate: 0.09, gst_registration_number: [number], gst_registered_from: [date], gst_display_mode: inclusive.
	•	All financial calculations and document generation read from config values. Single config change = GST applied across entire platform immediately.

Phase 3 — After Registration
	•	Price display: all prices must be GST-INCLUSIVE (Singapore law). Cook sets GST-exclusive price. Platform displays GST-inclusive price prominently, GST amount separately.
	•	Tax invoices replace receipts: add 'TAX INVOICE' title, GST registration number, GST rate, GST amount separately, GST-exclusive amount, GST-inclusive total. Simplified tax invoice (no buyer details required) for transactions under S$1,000 — most food orders qualify.
	•	Quarterly GST F5 return: Xero handles preparation when correctly configured. Accountant reviews and submits. Due: last day of month following quarter end.
	•	Input tax claims: GST paid on Xero subscription, legal fees (GST-registered firms), insurance — partially offsets compliance cost.
	•	Overseas Vendor Registration (OVR): Claude API from Anthropic (US company) — check if GST-registered in Singapore. If not: platform may need to self-account for GST on API costs under reverse charge. Accountant to advise. At S$30–100/month API cost, trivial amount but compliance obligation must be correct.

29I. Minimum Order Value — S$50
DECISION: Single minimum order value of S$50 across the board. No tiered thresholds. Simplicity chosen deliberately. Fully configurable in platform_config — zero code change to adjust.

Rationale
	•	Filters out low-value daily meal orders that create operational complexity without contributing to occasion-focused platform identity.
	•	Platform earns minimum S$2.00 floor on a S$10 order vs S$9.00 on a S$50 order — economics strongly favour higher threshold.
	•	Clear signal to cooks and customers: this is a platform for planned food orders, not spontaneous snacks.
	•	Simple to communicate, simple to enforce, simple to understand.

Commission Tier Interaction
	•	With S$50 minimum, effective commission tiers are: S$50–S$80 at 18%, S$81–S$150 at 15%, above S$150 at 12%.
	•	The 20% tier (up to S$30) is effectively dormant at standard minimum — only applies if minimum is temporarily lowered.

Tasting Portions — Exception
	•	Tasting portions (S$3–8) exempt from S$50 minimum — they are a platform-subsidised trust feature, not a commercial transaction.
	•	Tasting portion listings flagged with tasting_portion: true in database. Maximum price: S$8 (platform enforces ceiling to prevent abuse).

29J. Mobile App Strategy — PWA, Android and iOS
DECISION: Three-tier mobile strategy. PWA moved from Phase 3 to Phase 1 (mandatory at launch). Android native app at Phase 2. iOS native app at Phase 3. Home cooks use smartphones as primary device — mobile-first is not optional.

Why This Decision Was Made
	•	Home cook demographic (Malay aunties, Chinese grandmothers, Indian home bakers) overwhelmingly uses smartphone as primary and often only computing device.
	•	A mobile-responsive website requires navigating to a URL, logging in, waiting for page load. A PWA or native app opens instantly from the home screen.
	•	Cook use cases demand mobile: confirming orders while cooking (hands floury), checking earnings on MRT, uploading photos directly from camera roll, receiving push notifications at 11pm.
	•	Previous Phase 4 timeline was wrong — moved to Phase 1 (PWA) and Phase 2 (Android).

Tier 1 — PWA (Phase 1 — Mandatory at Launch)
	•	Installable on any smartphone home screen (iOS and Android) without App Store.
	•	Covers 90% of native app functionality: push notifications, camera access, offline cached content, home screen icon.
	•	Additional development time: 1–2 weeks on top of web build. Web app manifest + service worker.
	•	Cook recruitment message changes: 'visit our website' → 'download our app.'
	•	Push notifications via OneSignal from Phase 1 for all cook and customer notifications.

Tier 2 — Android Native App (Phase 2 — Weeks 9–20 of Build)
	•	Android before iOS: dominates home cook demographic (Samsung Galaxy A-series, Xiaomi — S$200–400 devices).
	•	APK sideloading: distribute directly to Founding Cook cohort via WhatsApp for 2-week beta before Play Store submission. No App Store approval needed.
	•	Technical approach: React Native with Expo. Reuses web business logic and API calls. Mobile-specific: Expo Camera, Expo Notifications, Expo ImagePicker, React Navigation.
	•	Additional development time: 6–8 weeks over web build.
	•	Google Play developer fee: US$25 one-time. Play Store review: 1–3 days. Live: Week 18–20 of build.
	•	Key Phase 2 mobile feature: one-tap order confirm/decline directly from push notification — materially better on native app than PWA.

Tier 3 — iOS Native App (Phase 3 — Month 5–6)
	•	iOS after Android: React Native codebase already written — iOS is a port, not a rebuild. Additional time: 3–4 weeks.
	•	TestFlight beta: distribute to Founding Cook cohort before App Store submission. Up to 10,000 external testers.
	•	App Store compliance: PayNow for food is NOT a digital good — exempt from Apple's in-app purchase requirement. Same legal basis as GrabFood, Deliveroo, Foodpanda.
	•	Apple Developer Program: US$99/year (~S$135). App Store review: 1–2 weeks. Live: Month 6.

App Store as Trust Signal and Marketing
	•	'Available on App Store and Google Play' on website gives credibility that a URL alone cannot provide.
	•	App Store search discovery: users searching 'home cooked food Singapore', 'birthday cake Singapore' in App Store may find platform directly.
	•	Regular app updates signal active platform development — both stores surface recently updated apps.

Cook-Specific Native App Features
	•	One-tap order confirm/decline from notification: Phase 2 Android, Phase 3 iOS.
	•	Biometric login: Face ID or fingerprint — no password entry. Critical for older cooks.
	•	Camera: one-tap photo from camera roll to listing upload — no file selection dialogue.
	•	Offline order queue: view pending orders without internet (HDB lifts, MRT tunnels).
	•	Home screen widget (Phase 4): '3 pending orders · S$280 this week' glanceable on home screen.
